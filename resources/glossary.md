# Glossary

Key terms used during the hackathon.

---

**Accessibility**
Making services usable by everyone, including people with disabilities. Government services must meet WCAG 2.2 AA standard.

**Back stage**
The parts of a service that users cannot see — internal processes, systems, staff actions, and policies that happen behind the scenes.

**Copilot**
An AI assistant. Today you are using two: M365 Copilot (for thinking and writing) and GitHub Copilot (for building HTML prototypes).

**Double diamond**
A design process model. The first diamond is about understanding the problem (discover and define). The second is about solving it (develop and deliver).

**Front stage**
The parts of a service that users can see and interact with — websites, letters, phone calls, face-to-face interactions.

**GOV.UK Design System**
A collection of components, patterns, and styles for building government services. It ensures services look and feel consistent.

**How Might We**
A question format used in design to reframe problems as opportunities. "How might we help citizens check their application status without phoning?"

**One thing per page**
A GOV.UK design pattern where each page asks users for one piece of information. It makes services easier to use, especially on mobile and for people with accessibility needs.

**Pain point**
A specific moment in a user's journey where they experience frustration, confusion, or difficulty.

**Persona**
A fictional but realistic person representing a group of users. Based on research and professional knowledge, not assumptions.

**Prompt**
An instruction you give to an AI tool. The more specific and contextual your prompt, the more useful the output.

**Prototype**
A working model of a service or part of a service. Today you are building HTML prototypes — web pages you can click through to demonstrate a concept.

**Semantic HTML**
HTML that uses elements for their meaning, not just their appearance. For example, using `<h1>` for main headings, `<label>` for form labels, and `<nav>` for navigation. It matters for accessibility and screen readers.

**Service blueprint**
A diagram showing how a service works across front-stage (what users see) and back-stage (what happens internally). It maps user actions, touchpoints, staff actions, and support processes.

**User journey**
The steps a person goes through when using a service, from start to finish. Includes what they do, think, and feel at each step.

**WCAG**
Web Content Accessibility Guidelines. The international standard for making web content accessible. Government services must meet WCAG 2.2 level AA.
