# Sample Problem Statement: Check the Progress of Your Application

This is an example of what a refined problem statement looks like after one or two rounds of prompting and refinement with M365 Copilot. It is not a first draft and it is not perfect — it is realistic output from the process.

---

## Current situation

When citizens submit an application to a government service — whether for a benefit, a licence, a permit, or a registration — they receive little or no information about what happens next. There is no way to check the status of their application online. The only option is to phone a contact centre.

Contact centre wait times are long. When citizens do get through, the information they receive is often vague, inconsistent, or out of date. Different agents give different answers about the same case. Citizens are left to guess whether their application is progressing, stuck, or lost.

## Who is affected

**Citizens** who have submitted an application and are waiting for a decision that affects their daily life — their income, their housing, their legal status, their ability to work. The wait creates anxiety, and the lack of information makes it worse. Citizens who are less digitally confident, who speak English as a second language, or who have accessibility needs are disproportionately affected because they have fewer ways to find information independently.

**Contact centre staff** who spend a significant proportion of their time answering "where is my application?" queries. These are avoidable contacts — the citizen does not need to speak to a person, they need to see a status. Staff find these calls frustrating because they often cannot provide a clear answer either.

**Caseworkers** whose processing time is interrupted by urgent status queries escalated from the contact centre. Time spent chasing updates is time not spent making decisions.

**Operational managers** who are managing backlogs, complaint volumes, and performance targets that are all made worse by the lack of transparency.

## Impact

- High volumes of avoidable contact driving up cost per transaction
- Citizen dissatisfaction and formal complaints increasing
- Staff morale affected by repetitive, low-value queries they cannot resolve well
- Processing times extended because caseworkers are pulled into status chasing
- Loss of public trust in the service

## What good looks like

Citizens can check the status of their application at any time, without phoning. They can see:
- Where their application is in the process
- Whether anything is needed from them
- When they can expect to hear back

When something changes — a decision is made, a document is needed, a deadline is approaching — citizens are told proactively rather than having to chase.

The service is simple enough that someone with low digital confidence can use it without help. It works on a phone. It does not require account creation or complex verification.

Contact centre staff spend their time on queries that genuinely need human judgement, not status checks. Caseworkers are not interrupted. Managers can see accurate, real-time data on application volumes and processing times.
