# Step 1: Frame the Problem

**Tool:** M365 Copilot

Open M365 Copilot — either through the Copilot app or at [copilot.microsoft.com](https://copilot.microsoft.com). Copy the prompt below and paste it in. Read what it gives you — then refine it.

## Prompt to copy

```
We are a team of user researchers, service designers, product managers, content designers, and business analysts working on improving a government service.

The service: Checking the progress of a government application after submission.

The problem: Once citizens submit an application, they have no visibility of progress. The only option is to phone a contact centre, which has long wait times and inconsistent information. Citizens do not know where their application is, whether anything is needed from them, or when they will hear back.

Users affected: Citizens waiting on decisions that affect their lives. Contact centre staff fielding avoidable status queries. Caseworkers interrupted by status chasing.

Help us write a structured problem statement covering:
- What is the current situation?
- Who is affected and how?
- What is the impact (on users, staff, and the organisation)?
- What does good look like?

Plain language. No jargon.
```

## What to look for

- Does it capture the citizen experience — the anxiety, the uncertainty, the lack of control?
- Does it mention the organisational cost — avoidable contact, staff time, inconsistency?
- Is "what good looks like" grounded in user needs, not technology?
- What would you add from your own professional experience?

## Now refine it

Try a follow-up prompt:

```
Make the "what good looks like" section more specific. Citizens should be able to check progress without phoning. They should get proactive updates when something changes or when action is needed from them.
```

This is the core loop: **prompt, review, challenge, refine.**
