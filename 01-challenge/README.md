# Challenge: Check the Progress of Your Application

This is a guided challenge. Every team works the same problem, following the same steps.

Steps 1–4 use M365 Copilot — you will frame a problem, create a persona, and map a service. Step 5 uses GitHub Copilot to generate your first web page.

Each step has a prompt to copy, guidance on what to look for, and a sample output. The goal is not perfection — it is getting comfortable with the tools and the cycle of prompt, review, challenge, refine.

Work through in order. Take the time you need — some steps will take longer than others.

## The problem: citizens cannot see where their application is

### The scenario

You work in a government department that processes applications from citizens. These might be licence applications, benefit claims, permit requests, registration renewals, case referrals — the specifics do not matter. What matters is the experience citizens have after they submit.

### What is going wrong

Once a citizen submits an application, they enter a black hole. They have no visibility of where it is, what stage it has reached, whether anything is needed from them, or when they might hear back.

The only option is to phone a contact centre — which is often engaged, has long wait times, and where the agent may not have access to up-to-date information anyway.

This creates:

- **Anxiety for citizens** waiting on decisions that affect their housing, income, legal situation, or daily life
- **Avoidable contact** — a significant proportion of calls are simply "where is my application?"
- **Inconsistent information** — different agents give different answers about the same case
- **Staff time consumed** by status queries instead of processing
- **Complaints and loss of trust** when citizens feel ignored or unable to get a straight answer

Citizens do not understand the internal process and should not need to. They want to know three things: **where is it, is anything needed from me, and when will I hear back.**

### Who is affected

- Citizens waiting on a decision that affects their life
- Contact centre staff fielding avoidable status queries
- Caseworkers whose processing time is eaten by chasing
- Operational managers tracking backlogs, complaints, and performance

### Why this problem

Every government department has a version of this. Whether it is a court hearing, a benefit claim, a licence, a tribunal appeal, a visa, or a registration — the pattern is the same. It is one of the most common causes of avoidable contact in government.
