# Principles for Using AI Today

These principles apply to how you use AI tools during this hackathon — and to how you think about AI in your work more broadly.

---

## 1. AI assists, you decide

AI generates options. You apply professional judgement. The AI does not know your users, your context, or your constraints. You do. Treat its output as a first draft from a capable but uninformed colleague.

## 2. Start with the user need, not the technology

The problem comes first. Define the user need before you open an AI tool. AI is a means to explore and express solutions faster — it is not the starting point.

## 3. Challenge the output

AI is confident, not correct. It will present plausible-sounding content that may be wrong, shallow, or generic. Interrogate it. Ask yourself: would a User Researcher recognise this persona? Would a Content Designer accept this language? Would a Service Designer believe this blueprint?

## 4. Iterate, do not accept

First output is a draft. Refine it, redirect it, or reject it and start again. The value comes from the conversation — prompt, review, challenge, refine — not from the first response.

## 5. Be transparent

Be clear about what AI produced and what humans shaped. In your presentations today and in your work more broadly, do not present AI-generated content as if it came from research, analysis, or professional judgement unless it has been through that lens.

## 6. Accessibility is non-negotiable

AI-generated content and code must meet the same accessibility standards as anything else. Check colour contrast, heading hierarchy, form labels, and screen reader compatibility. Use the accessibility prompts in this repo to help.

## 7. Protect data

Use fictional examples only. Do not put real user data, case data, personal information, or sensitive organisational details into AI prompts. Everything you type into an AI tool today should be something you would be comfortable sharing publicly.
