# Step 3: Map the Current Service

**Tool:** M365 Copilot

Generate a service blueprint — then use your team's collective experience to challenge it.

## Prompt to copy

```
Service: Checking the progress of a government application after submission
Journey stages: Application submitted, citizen wants an update, citizen tries to find information, citizen contacts the service, citizen gets an answer (or not), citizen continues waiting

Create a service blueprint with these layers:
1. FRONT STAGE - What the citizen does
2. FRONT STAGE - Touchpoints (website, phone, letter, email)
3. FRONT STAGE - What staff do that the citizen can see
4. BACK STAGE - Internal staff actions the citizen cannot see
5. SUPPORT PROCESSES - Systems, databases, policies, third parties

Format as a table with a column for each journey stage.
Then list the top 3-5 pain points and where they occur.
```

## What to look for

- Does the back-stage look realistic? AI tends to oversimplify internal processes.
- Where is the biggest gap between what citizens need and what the service provides?
- Where does avoidable contact happen?

## Now refine it

```
Add a row for what the citizen is thinking and feeling at each stage. Mark where avoidable contact occurs.
```
