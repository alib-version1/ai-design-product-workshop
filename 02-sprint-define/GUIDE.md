# Sprint 1: Define Your Own Problem

## Your brief

You have worked a shared problem together in the challenge. Now pick one that matters to you.

Think about the services you have worked on, the problems you have seen, the user needs you know are not being met. Pick something real. The best problems for today are ones where:

- There is a clear user need that is not being met well
- You can describe it in one sentence
- You could show the concept in 3–5 screens
- Your team has enough collective knowledge to define it without doing new research

## How to spend your time

**First 20 minutes — choose and frame.** Each person writes down one problem from professional experience. Discuss. Pick one. Use the prompts in [prompts/problem-and-research.md](../prompts/problem-and-research.md) to sharpen it with M365 Copilot.

**Next 20 minutes — persona and assumptions.** Use the persona and assumptions prompts in [prompts/problem-and-research.md](../prompts/problem-and-research.md). Challenge what the AI gives you — does it match what you have seen in research, casework, delivery?

**Final 20 minutes — map the service and pick your intervention.** Use the prompts in [prompts/service-mapping.md](../prompts/service-mapping.md). Identify pain points. Choose the area your prototype will address this afternoon. Use the [content and language prompts](../prompts/content-and-language.md) to check and sharpen your problem statement wording.

Use the [team worksheet](../templates/team-worksheet.md) to capture your work as you go.

## How each role contributes

- **User Researchers**: Ground everything in user experience. What evidence exists? What are we assuming? Does this persona ring true?
- **Service Designers**: See the whole journey. Where does this problem sit end-to-end? What is the back-stage reality?
- **Product Managers**: Frame the strategic case. Who cares? What outcome does success deliver? What do we cut?
- **Content Designers**: Listen for language problems. Where does the service confuse people? Where is plain English missing?
- **Interaction Designers**: Identify touchpoints. Where do users interact? What patterns apply?
- **Business Analysts**: Map the rules and processes. What constraints shape this? What are the edge cases?
- **Delivery Managers**: Keep the team focused. What is realistic for this afternoon?
- **Directors and Deputy Directors**: Strategic lens. Is this the right problem? What would make this compelling to fund?

## What you need by the end of Sprint 1

- A problem statement your team can explain in 30 seconds
- At least one persona
- A service map showing where you plan to intervene
- Agreement on 3–5 screens to prototype
