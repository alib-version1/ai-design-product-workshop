# Getting Started with M365 Copilot

M365 Copilot is an AI assistant you can use through the Copilot app or the web at [copilot.microsoft.com](https://copilot.microsoft.com). You give it instructions in plain language and it generates content you can review and refine.

## Where to find it

Open the Copilot app on your device, or go to [copilot.microsoft.com](https://copilot.microsoft.com) in your browser.

## Five things that make the difference

1. **Be specific.** "Structured problem statement about citizens checking application progress" works. "Write about a problem" does not.

2. **Give context.** Tell it your role, what you are working on, and what format you need.

3. **Iterate.** The first output is a starting point, not a finished product.

4. **Challenge it.** You know your domain better than the AI does. If something does not ring true, say so.

5. **Use your judgement.** A User Researcher knows whether a persona is realistic. A Content Designer knows whether the language is right. That expertise is yours.

## Common pitfalls

- Vague prompts that do not give enough context
- Accepting the first output without reading critically
- Not providing enough detail about your service and users
- Expecting it to know things about your specific service or department

## Try it now

Open the Copilot app or go to [copilot.microsoft.com](https://copilot.microsoft.com). Type:

> "Write a paragraph explaining what a service blueprint is and why it matters, for someone who has never seen one."

Read what comes back. Then type:

> "Shorter. Plainer language."

That is the loop: prompt, review, refine.
